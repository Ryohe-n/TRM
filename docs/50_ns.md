- Training
- Transmitter
The transmitter does not require any special training sequences, so the logic just sends the desired coefficient by programming desired values from the registers during initial boot.
- Receiver
The receiver contains an adaptation circuit to adjust the internal equalizer controls to optimize the signal characteristics based on the incoming data.
- The EQ training sequence is performed as part of the transition into high-speed mode while
receiving the training data. For design flexibility, the controller uses the register bit below to determine whether the EQ training (start/adaptation steps) is performed.

10G Ethernet Controller Functional Description Additionally, the controller has options to perform EQ training during certain L1 events, periodically during high-speed mode and upon recovery entry in case of electrical errors. In order for equalization training to optimize the signal properly during initial training and subsequent periodic training events, it is important that high-speed data is present at the input of the receiver. This requires that the equalization event is not triggered until the transition to high speed has occurred and that the equalization event is stopped/aborted as quickly as possible when transitioning out of high-speed data.
- Initial
To start a clean adaptation, the EQ engine should be reset before each adaptation cycle.
